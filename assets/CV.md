## Research focus

PhD candidate at the *University of Turin* (Functional Genomics Unit, Italy) in computational cancer biology.

My research focuses on **chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)** and **Richter transformation (RT)**. I use multi-omics integration — RNA-seq, CUT&Tag, and DNA methylation — to study how B-cell receptor (BCR) stereotyped subsets and *NOTCH1* mutation status shape transcriptional and regulatory programs. I am especially interested in how biological context changes the interpretation of molecular signals, and in making defensible analytical decisions under complex, multi-batch study designs.

## Research experience

### PhD Researcher (Bioinformatics) — University of Turin, Dept. of Medical Sciences (2024–present)

- **Main PhD project — BCR stereotypy × *NOTCH1* in CLL.** Quantify genotype-linked transcriptional and regulatory programs across stereotyped subsets (IGHV4-34, IGHV1-69, IGHV3-21), comparing ***NOTCH1*-mutant vs wild-type** contexts, and translate signals into mechanistic hypotheses and testable predictions.

- **Multi-batch RNA-seq design and QC ownership.** Managed a **3-batch** study under real constraints, including dropouts and uneven libraries; rebuilt analysis logic when designs became partially invalid, enforced QC gates, and used batch-aware modeling to preserve biological interpretability.

- **Multi-omics integration.** Integrate bulk RNA-seq with CUT&Tag (TFs, H3K27ac) and ONT DNA methylation to connect regulatory state with genotype/context and prioritize perturbation-ready hypotheses.

- **External collaborations.** Ongoing analysis collaboration with CLL groups at **IRCCS San Raffaele Hospital (HSR, Milan)**, including study design support and bulk RNA-seq analysis.

- **Data stewardship.** Controlled-access submissions to EGA, including metadata, manifests, checksums, Crypt4GH encryption, and DAC coordination.

- **Analytical infrastructure.** Built and maintain practical tools used in analysis workflows, including **TranscripTools (R)** and **RNAseq-DB-explorer (Shiny)**.
